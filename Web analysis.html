Analyzing Website Performance Metrics

The analysis covered data from January to October 10, 2024, focusing on user behavior, engagement, and eCommerce metrics. The goal was to understand trends, identify key areas for improvement, and provide recommendations to optimize user experience and conversion rates.

Performance Overview

Active Users: 17.2K (down 12.8% from the previous period)

Pageviews per User: 4.5 (decreased by 25.1%)

Engagement Time: 1 minute, 6 seconds (dropped 40%)

New Users: 73% of traffic (up 12.8%)

Understanding User Behavior: Digging into Trends

A crucial part of the project involved understanding user demographics and behaviors. Key user trends, such as geographic distribution, device usage, and traffic sources, were examined.

Geography: The website attracted visitors from various regions, with the United States, Canada, and India being the top sources. This indicates a need to tailor content or campaigns to these key markets.

Device Usage: A significant 59.1% of users accessed the site via mobile devices, emphasizing the importance of mobile optimization. Ensuring a seamless mobile experience could help improve engagement metrics and conversion rates.

Traffic Sources: Direct traffic accounted for 65.3% of sessions, followed by organic search (16.9%) and email campaigns. The October 2024 newsletter contributed nearly 4% of traffic, showcasing the effectiveness of targeted email outreach.

Event Analysis: Mapping User Actions on the Website

GA4's event tracking data provided insights into user behavior on the site:

  • Page Views: 78,068 instances, representing 26.5% of all events.
  • Viewing Item Lists: 69,971 occurrences (23.7%), indicating interest in product categories.
  • Sessions: Over 23,000 recorded, showing consistent traffic but raising questions about why engagement metrics were dropping.

eCommerce Insights: Uncovering Opportunities for Growth

The eCommerce section revealed some concerns:

  • Total Purchases: 249 transactions, a 56.3% decrease from the previous period.
  • Revenue: $43.4K (down 53.9%), though the average order value increased by 5.6% to $174.
  • Conversion Rate: The siteâ€™s conversion rate fell to 1.26%, prompting a closer look into potential friction points in the checkout process.

Recommendations include simplifying the checkout process, conducting A/B testing on checkout pages, and refining product descriptions to be more compelling.

Challenges Faced and Problem-Solving Approaches

Several challenges required strategic thinking and problem-solving:

  • Interpreting Data Fluctuations: The drop in engagement time and conversions suggested potential issues with user retention. Possible causes included seasonal trends, changes in content, or site updates.
  • Segmenting Data for Deeper Insights: Breaking down data by device type, country, and session source helped uncover more granular insights and guided tailored recommendations.

Key Takeaways and Recommendations

Based on the analysis, key takeaways include:

  • Improve Engagement: Invest in interactive content, multimedia, or gamification elements to keep users on the site longer.
  • Mobile-First Strategy: Given that nearly 60% of traffic comes from mobile devices, prioritize mobile optimization.
  • Leverage Email Campaigns: Expand email marketing efforts to drive additional traffic and conversions.
  • Optimize the Conversion Funnel: Simplify the checkout process and offer incentives like free shipping or discounts for first-time customers.

Conclusion: Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrated skills in data analysis and the ability to translate data into actionable business strategies. The insights and recommendations aim to improve user experience and drive business growth.
